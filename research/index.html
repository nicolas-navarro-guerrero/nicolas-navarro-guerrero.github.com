Embodied R&D for Safe, Adaptive Robot Dexterity

I advocate holistic robot development that integrates hardware and software, in line with embodied and developmental robotics. My long-term vision is to create machine learning methods for increasingly autonomous, safer, and human-aware systems in industrial and assistive settings.

I focus on methods that enable robots to perceive, understand, and act robustly in complex real-world environments, with robustness, adaptability, and transparency at the core. I am especially interested in academic-industrial partnerships to deliver knowledge-intensive R&D solutions for socially relevant challenges.

This page presents my research portfolio through four complementary pillars. Each pillar reflects a core dimension of robot development for haptic perception, dexterous manipulation, and safe interaction around people.
